Exercises for Better and Deep Sleep

Exercise 1: 4-7-8 Breathing Method

This simple but powerful breathing method promotes calmness and relaxation. It helps activate the parasympathetic nervous system.

  1. Place your tongue behind your upper front teeth.
  2. Exhale fully through your mouth with a “whoosh” sound.
  3. Inhale quietly through your nose for 4 seconds.
  4. Hold your breath for 7 seconds.
  5. Exhale fully through your mouth for 8 seconds.
  6. Repeat this cycle 3–4 times.

Exercise 2: Yoga, Meditation & Mindfulness

These practices reduce stress and improve sleep quality by calming the mind and body.

  • Yoga uses breathing and movement to release tension.
  • Meditation boosts melatonin and prepares the brain for sleep.
  • Mindfulness reduces bedtime anxiety and enhances sleep quality.
  • Practicing them regularly leads to deeper, better sleep.

Exercise 3: Get on a Consistent Sleep Schedule

Sticking to regular sleep and wake times can align your body’s internal clock.

  • Your circadian rhythm regulates sleep and alertness.
  • Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day.
  • Aim for 7–9 hours of sleep nightly.
  • Give yourself 30–45 minutes to wind down before bed.

Exercise 4: Change Your Eating Habits

What you eat and when you eat affects sleep quality and digestion.

  • Avoid large meals within 1 hour of bedtime.
  • Minimize refined carbs which may cause sleep issues.
  • Focus on whole grains, dairy, fruits, and vegetables.
  • Allow at least 2–3 hours after eating before lying down.

Exercise 5: Listen to Relaxing Music

Music can calm your nervous system and improve sleep onset and quality.

  • Choose soft, slow music before bed.
  • Studies show improved sleep in those who listen to music.
  • Avoid loud or fast-paced music that may overstimulate you.
