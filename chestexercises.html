CHEST EXERCISES

Exercise 1: Pushups

The push-up builds both upper-body and core strength. It burns calories and boosts metabolism by building muscle.

  1. Get down on all fours with hands slightly wider than shoulders.
  2. Straighten arms and legs.
  3. Lower body until chest nearly touches the floor.
  4. Pause, then push yourself back up.
  5. Repeat.

Exercise 2: Dumbbell Bench Press

A variation of the barbell press, this builds chest mass while improving shoulder stability.

  1. Pick up dumbbells with a neutral grip and sit on the bench.
  2. Lay back and position weights near your chest.
  3. Press up to lockout.
  4. Lower slowly under control.
  5. Repeat for desired reps.

Exercise 3: Barbell Bench Press

This power-building move targets multiple upper body muscles and is a staple for strength programs.

  1. Lie flat and grip bar just outside shoulders.
  2. Pinch shoulder blades and unrack the bar.
  3. Lower to sternum with control.
  4. Press back up using leg drive and core tension.
  5. Repeat for reps.

Exercise 4: Dumbbell Pull-Over

This exercise stretches and works both chest and lats, especially effective on an incline bench.

  1. Grasp dumbbell and lay with upper back on bench, hips low.
  2. Extend arms overhead and lower dumbbell behind head.
  3. Keep elbows slightly bent and stretch chest.
  4. Return to start and repeat for reps.

Exercise 5: Chest Dip

Excellent for targeting the lower chest. Also works triceps and front delts.

  3. Press up powerfully and repeat.

Exercise 6: Cable Crossovers

Builds definition and stretches the chest fully with focused contraction.

  1. Adjust cable handles to highest setting.
  2. Step forward with one foot and brace core.
  3. Pull arms forward in a wide arc until they meet at chest level.
  4. Pause and squeeze, then return with control.
