Breakfast

Here's an example breakfast meal:

  • Scrambled eggs

    Eggs have been a dietary staple since time immemorial and there’s good reason for their continued presence in our menus and meals. One large boiled egg has about 77 calories and contains: Vitamins A, B5, B12, D, E, K, B6 Folate Phosphorus Selenium Calcium Zinc Six grams of protein Five grams of healthy fats “Eggs are a good source of protein (both whites/yolk). They also contain heart-healthy unsaturated fats and are a great source of important nutrients, such as vitamin B6, B12 and vitamin D,” says Kurt Hong, MD, an internal medicine specialist at Keck Medicine of USC.

  • Whole wheat toast

    Whole wheat bread offers a number of heath benefits, from boosting digestive and heart health to lowering the risk of several serious conditions.

    1. Promotes Healthy Digestion
    2. May Reduce the Risk of Digestive Cancer
    3. Boosts Heart Health
    4. May Reduce the Risk of Type 2 Diabetes

  • Avocado slices

    Avocados are a source of key nutrients, including healthy fats and fiber. They also contain anti-inflammatory and antioxidant compounds and may help reduce heart disease risk.

    1. Beneficial for gut health
    2. May help reduce heart disease risk factors
    3. A rich source of antioxidant and anti-inflammatory compounds
    4. May help promote a healthy body weight

  • Fruit salad

    Fruit salad is a dish consisting of various kinds of fruit, sometimes served in a liquid, either their juices or a syrup. In different forms, fruit salad can be served as an appetizer or a side salad. When served as an appetizer, a fruit salad is sometimes known as a fruit cocktail, or fruit cup.

    1. Weight loss
    2. Healthy nutrition profile
    3. Improved digestion

Lunch

Here's an example lunch meal:

  • Grilled chicken breast

    Chicken is the most popular poultry in the world - and for good reason! It’s low in calories, low in fat and has a high nutritional value. Grilled chicken is also a great source of protein. People who get enough of this nutrient are more likely to maintain muscle mass and support a healthy metabolism. No wonder why chicken can follow you from infancy to adulthood! If you’re looking to add more flavor to your diet, you’ll be happy to know that chicken breasts are versatile and inexpensive. You can use them in tacos, soups, sandwiches, salads or as a main entree paired with vegetables and whole grain bread.

    1. Increase Your Protein Supply
    2. Lose Weight
    3. Support Healthy Bones
    4. Boost Your Immunity

  • Brown rice

    Brown rice is a rich source of phenols and flavonoids, two types of antioxidants that help reduce damage to cells and reduce the risk of premature aging. Brown rice also provides you with many vitamins and minerals:

    1. Improved Heart Health
    2. Better Weight Control
    3. Reduced Risk of Diabetes

  • Steamed vegetables

    1. Keeps the vitamins and minerals intact
    2. Requires no use of oil
    3. Makes the food easy to digest
    4. Helps keep cholesterol in check

  • Greek salad

    Greek salad is low-calorie and nutrient-dense, also providing you with a healthy dose of vitamins A and C, as well as potassium and iron. It also contains phytonutrients and antioxidants, which reduces the risk of health problems such as heart disease and cancer

    1. Rich in Nutrients
    2. Lowers Blood Pressure
    3. Reduces Inflammation
    4. Supports Weight Loss
    5. Improves Bone and Heart Health

Dinner

Here's an example dinner meal:

  • Baked salmon

    Salmon is an oily fish that's typically classified by the ocean in which it is located. In the Pacific, they are considered part of the genus Oncorhynchus, and in the Atlantic, they belong to the genus Salmo.

    1. May support a healthy heart
    2. anti-inflammatory
    3. May support brain function

  • Quinoa

    Quinoa is a plant based whole grain that is rapidly growing in popularity due to its many health benefits.

    1. The fiber in quinoa can also help with cholesterol and blood sugar levels, lowering your risk of diabetes and heart disease.
    2. Quinoa is rich in antioxidants, which can prevent damage to your heart and other organs. A diet high in antioxidants has been linked with a decreased risk of heart disease.
    3. Quinoa is a great source of fiber. Fiber can prevent or treat constipation and may lower your risk of intestinal cancers.

  • Roasted sweet potatoes

    Sweet potatoes are nutritious, packing a good amount of vitamin A, vitamin C, and manganese into each serving. They also have anticancer properties and may promote immune function and offer other health benefits.

    1. Highly nutritious
    2. Promote gut health
    3. Have cancer-fighting properties
    4. Support healthy vision

  • green beans

    Green beans, or string beans, are rich in vital nutrients and vitamins K, C, and A.

    1. May Help Combat Cancer
    2. Help Control Diabetes
    3. Promote Gastrointestinal Health
    4. Promote Gastrointestinal Health

Snacks

Here are some healthy snack options:

  • Carrot sticks with hummus

    Traditional hummus is made from chickpeas and sesame oil, so it contains plant protein as well as healthy fat and fiber. Carrots are a good source of vitamin A and potassium, so the pairing makes a balanced and nutritious snack.1 As far as calories go, one-half cup of hummus has about 200-300 calories,2 while eight baby carrots have only about 30 calories.

    1. It has protein and healthy fat and heart-healthy soluble fiber
    2. Improve Blood Sugar Regulation
    3. May Help Regulate Appetite

  • Greek yogurt with berries

    Greek yogurt is a thick, creamy yogurt that can add flavor and texture to foods. But, it may also have other benefits, such as boosting bone health, building muscle mass, and lowering the risk of type 2 diabetes and high blood pressure.

    1. Improving bone health
    2. Reducing appetite and hunger
    3. Boosting metabolism
    4. Building muscle mass
    5. Lowering blood pressure

  • Almonds or mixed nuts

    Eating nuts as part of a healthy diet may be good for the heart. Nuts contain unsaturated fatty acids and other nutrients. And they're a great snack food — inexpensive, easy to store and easy to pack when you're on the go.

    1. Improve artery health
    2. Reduce inflammation related to heart disease
    3. Decrease the risk of blood clots, which can lead to heart attacks and strokes
    4. Lower the risk of high blood pressure
    5. Lower unhealthy cholesterol levels, specifically low-density lipoprotein (LDL or "bad") cholesterol and triglycerides, which can clog arteries

  • Rice cakes with peanut butter

    Top multigrain rice cakes with creamy peanut butter for a nutritious snack with protein and fiber to keep you feeling full.Rice cakes and peanut butter make a great mid-morning snack that looks after your cells and DNA, helps detox your liver, gives you energy and more! In the US, "peanut butter" must contain at least 90% peanuts, otherwise it is called "peanut spread".

    1. Low Calorie
    2. Low Fat
    3. Gluten-free
    4. Low Sodium
