LOWER BODY

Exercise 1: Jumping Jacks

Jumping jacks are a calisthenics exercise to warm up and improve endurance.

  1. Stand up straight, arms at sides, feet shoulder-width apart.
  2. Jump and extend arms overhead.
  3. Extend legs outward as you jump.
  4. Return to starting position.
  5. Repeat.

Exercise 2: Squats

Squats strengthen hips, glutes, quads, hamstrings, and improve posture.

  1. Stand with feet wider than hip-width, toes forward.
  2. Bend knees, drive hips back, and open knees slightly.
  3. Lower into a seated position, chest up.
  4. Go as low as parallel to the floor.
  5. Press through heels to return to standing.

Exercise 3: Side-Lying Left Leg Lift

This move targets the hip abductors, glutes, and core for lower body strength and control.

  1. Lie on your right side, legs extended and stacked.
  2. Support head and brace with your left hand.
  3. Raise left leg slowly, keeping hips stacked.
  4. Lower gently to starting position.
  5. Repeat 10–12 times, then switch sides.

Exercise 4: Side-Lying Right Leg Lift

Activates hip abductors and stabilizing muscles to support knee and hip mobility.

  1. Lie on your left side, legs extended and stacked.
  2. Support head with left arm, right hand on hip or floor.
  3. Lift right leg slowly and pause at the top.
  4. Lower gently and repeat.
  5. Do 10–12 reps, then switch sides.

Exercise 5: Dumbbell Lunge

Strengthens glutes, hamstrings, and core. Builds unilateral leg power and balance.

  1. Stand with dumbbells in each hand, arms at sides.
  2. Step forward with right leg into a lunge.
  3. Push through right heel to return to start.
  4. Alternate legs or repeat on one side before switching.
  5. Do 5–7 reps per leg.

Exercise 6: Bulgarian Split Squat

Targets quads, glutes, and core while improving balance and flexibility.

  1. Place rear foot on a bench, hold a dumbbell at chest level.
  2. Lower into a lunge, keeping spine tall and hips square.
  3. Push through front heel to return to start.
  4. Repeat for 7–10 reps per leg.
