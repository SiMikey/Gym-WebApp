Exercise 1: Jumping Jacks

Jumping jacks are a classic full-body warm-up exercise that improves cardiovascular endurance and coordination.

  1. Stand tall with arms at your sides and feet shoulder-width apart.
  2. Jump while raising your arms overhead and spreading your legs wide.
  3. Return to the starting position.
  4. Repeat continuously.

Exercise 2: Inclined Pushups

An elevated version of a standard push-up that reduces difficulty while improving upper-body strength.

  1. Place hands on a raised surface, feet on the floor.
  2. Keep body straight and core tight.
  3. Lower until chest touches the surface.
  4. Push back up with control.

Exercise 3: Knee Pushups

A beginner-friendly push-up variation that builds upper-body strength with less strain.

  1. Kneel on the floor and place hands shoulder-width apart.
  2. Engage your abs and lower your torso to the floor.
  3. Push up to starting position.
  4. Repeat with steady control.

Exercise 4: Pushups

A fundamental bodyweight exercise for building chest, triceps, shoulders, and core strength.

  1. Start in plank with hands slightly wider than shoulders.
  2. Lower until chest nearly touches floor.
  3. Push back up to starting position.
  4. Repeat for reps.

Exercise 5: Wide Arm Pushups

Targets the chest muscles more than traditional pushups by increasing the horizontal distance between the hands.

  1. Place hands wider than shoulders, body straight.
  2. Lower your body slowly keeping your back aligned.
  3. Push up to return to starting position.
  4. Repeat for reps.

Exercise 6: Cobra Stretch

Excellent yoga pose that stretches the chest, strengthens the spine, and enhances flexibility.

  1. Lie face down with hands under shoulders.
  2. Press into your palms and lift chest upward.
  3. Use lower back to deepen the stretch.
  4. Look slightly forward, hold, and lower gently.
